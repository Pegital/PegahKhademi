In this project, the usage of cellular automata is intended to produce new architectural forms while also promoting a better comprehension of the design process itself. Through a system in which advanced global behaviors emerge from local interactions between elements (cells), designers can investigate a wide range of design options that are naturally tailored to contextual and functional constraints.

To conduct a comprehensive structural analysis of our building structure under various loads, including wind, and to ensure seamless integration with Karamba3D for Finite Element Analysis (FEA), we first carefully arranged the geometric elements (beams, columns, slabs, and supports) into their appropriate layers within Rhino. This organizational approach not only made the workflow more efficient, but also set up our model for a smoother transition into the analysis stage.
