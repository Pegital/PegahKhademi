This project investigates robotic collaboration and AI-assisted design as an integrated creative process. Beginning with foundational pick-and-place and sorting experiments, the research established an understanding of robotic behavior and control. It then advanced to AI-generated robotic code based on conceptual design intentions rather than literal instructions. In its final phase, the robot was repositioned from a construction tool to a design partner, engaging in an iterative workflow supported by AI visualization. The project proposes a shift from automation toward human–AI–robot collaboration in architectural design.
